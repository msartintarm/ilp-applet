Case Study 3:
Spatial Architecture Scheduling

Hardware specialization has emerged as an important way to sustain performance improvements of microprocessors to address transistor energy efficiency challenges and general purpose processing's inefficiencies. The spatial architecture paradigm is to map large regions of computation to the hardware, as a static scheduler integrated into the compilation toolchain. The resultant mappings affect execution latency, and exploitable throughput, of the program - hence it is advantageous to use a scheduler that takes these factors into account.
In ILP, the properties common to spatial architectures are broken into a set of constraints encompassing responsibilities of the generalized scheduler. Several architectures have additional responsibilities in addition to the common set. All responsibilities are factored into a single linear objective function, which is minimized. The solver cuts off when a solution is within 10% of optimality.
